Locro Argentino

The Argentine Creole locro is a dish made from white corn, beans and squash or pumpkin. It requires a long cooking time, so it allows the use of tough cuts of meat such as brisket and it allows for different versions by varying the ingredients.

It has its origins in the time of the Spanish conquest of America and a rich tradition that reflects the fusion of both cultures. Due to its history and what it represents, it became a typical food of the national dates of Argentina.

Ingredients:
  • 500 grams of broken white corn
  • 500 grams of lima beans
  • 4 Knorr vegetable bouillon cubes
  • 2 liters of water
  • 250 grams of pork breast
  • 500 grams of pork feet
  • 125 grams of smoked bacon
  • 500 grams of veal brisket
  • 125 grams of chorizo colorado
  • 125 grams of pork chorizo
  • 500 grams of pumpkin
  • 125 grams of red bell pepper
  • 125 grams of green onion
  • 125 grams of leeks
  • 125 grams of carrot
  • 1 teaspoon of hot paprika
  • ground pepper and salt to taste
Salsa picante:
  • 100 grams of chopped green onion
  • 1 cup of oil
  • Paprika and ground chili to taste
  • 1/2 cup of water
Steps:
  1. First, the beans and corn have to be hydrated for at least 12 hours. So it is necessary to soak them
  2. Prepare the broth with water and the vegetable cubes separately.
  3. The pork breast, the skirt and the bacon are cut into cubes, the skin is removed from the red chorizo ​​and the pork chorizo. It is recommended to cut it into pieces.
  4. Chop the bell pepper, leek and green onion. Then the pumpkin and carrots are also cut into small or medium pieces, depending on preference.
  5. In a pot, begin to sauté the bacon. Then the pork breast and brisket are added until golden brown. You can add the hot paprika right there.
  6. It is time to incorporate all the vegetables, except the pumpkin. Chorizos and pork legs are added.
  7. Now the corn, the beans and the pumpkin are integrated together with the already made broth until it covers the entire preparation.
  8. Finally, cook over low heat for approximately 90 to 120 minutes and that's it. You already have the best homemade locro, a dish that has a long history.
